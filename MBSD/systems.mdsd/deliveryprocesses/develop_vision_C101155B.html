Steps
Characterize current situation
Here, you briefly examine the current situation or system to discover what the motivators for change might be. The current business or operational environment and the current response to this are studied and described. Aspects of interest are:
  • Current capabilities and how well they are performed
  • Current interfaces
  • Current non-functional characteristics, such as physical attributes, environmental tolerance and impact, and quality attributes.
Any of these aspects might give rise to concerns about the adequacy of the current solution.
Identify Constraints to be Imposed on the System - Systems Engineering Note
In the systems engineering case, the list of potential sources and questions to ask grows longer:
  • Physical - How are the physical characteristics of the system constrained? Are there mass, size, or shape constraints, for example?
  • Qualities - Are there reliability or maintainability or other "ility" requirements, for example?
  • Interfaces - Must the system maintain compatibility with existing interfaces?
Define Features of the System - Systems Engineering Note
In this step, you synthesize a description of the new (or modified) system in terms of:
  • Features or capabilities (as described above) and how well they are to be performed.
  • Important system operational scenarios - these are behavioral descriptions of how the system will respond to stimuli in its environment, and interact with its actors.
  • The impact of the system on various stakeholders - interface changes, procedural changes, staff changes (skills, numbers, training requirements).
Evaluate Your Results - Systems Engineering Note

Use this step to make a concrete analysis and record of the advantages and disadvantages of the proposal, and the rationale for the decisions.
