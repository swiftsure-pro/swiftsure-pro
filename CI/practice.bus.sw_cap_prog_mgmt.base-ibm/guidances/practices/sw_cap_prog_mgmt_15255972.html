Purpose

It provides a framework for assessing, managing, and improving software delivery capabilities within an organization.

Adoption of this practice helps you:

  • Identify the organization's software delivery strengths and weaknesses
  • Identify improvements needed in order to meet operational objectives
  • Develop a roadmap of incremental improvements with a clear adoption strategy
  • Monitor performance following implementation and adjust as needed
  • Manage the overall capability improvement program

  • Managing Performance Through Measurement - leverages the use and analysis of data from the Performance Measurement System to monitor capability improvements
  • Project Management practices - Plan, monitor, and control improvement projects
How to read this practice

The best way to read this practice is to first familiarize yourself with its overall structure: what is in it and how it is organized.

The best place to start is with the Key Concepts for the practice: those concepts that are critical to understand in order to adopt the practice:

  • Process Area
  • Pilot Project
  • Managing Organizational Change

Once you understand the key concepts, you can turn your attention to the Work Products produced by the practice:

  • Development Organization Assessment
  • Adoption Plan
  • Enterprise Tools

Then you can review the practices Tasks:

  • Assess the Organization
  • Select and Acquire Tools
  • Plan Adoption
  • Monitor Capability Improvement
  • Manage the Capability Improvement Program

From the work products and the tasks, you can access applicable guidance: guidelines and tool mentors associated with each task provide details about how to perform the task. Templates and checklists associated with the work products guide you in their completion and evaluation. You can also access the guidance provided by the practice directly, via the Guidance folder.
