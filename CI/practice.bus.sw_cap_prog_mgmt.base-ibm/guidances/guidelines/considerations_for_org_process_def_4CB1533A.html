Guideline: Considerations for Organizational Process Definition by Process Area
This guideline describes organizational process definition considerations by process area and can help you identify current gaps in your process and tool coverage.
Relationships
Related Elements
  • Assess the Organization
Main Description

It is helpful to compare your process against some standard such as the IBM Rational Unified Process, individual practices from the IBM Practice Library or to an improvement approach such as CMMI for Development. This can help you identify any gaps or areas of insufficient coverage. However, it is important to avoid implementing process improvements just to cover gaps. Confirm there is an organizational need (the process will help you achieve some operational objective) before implementing process improvements.

The following guidelines provide considerations for key process areas of software delivery. They will help you define practices (process assets) for your organization that are aligned with industry best practices and improvement models

  • Defining Practices for Risk Management
  • Defining Practices for Configuration and Change Management
  • Defining Practices for Project Management
  • Defining Practices for Requirements Management
  • Defining Practices for Requirements Definition
