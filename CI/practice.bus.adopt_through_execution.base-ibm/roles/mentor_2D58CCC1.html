Role: Mentor
A Mentor is an experienced practitioner who splits his or her time between their own project work and mentoring project teams in new software development capabilities.
Role Sets: Software Capability Improvement
Relationships
Primary Performs
  • Provide Enterprise Tool Adoption Support
  • Provide Method Adoption Support
Additionally Performs
  • Develop Mentors
  • Establish the Mentoring Program
  • Manage the Mentoring Program
Modifies
  • Mentoring Action Plan
    Main Description

    This role has the ability to raise the expertise of less experienced practitioners. Key responsibilities include:

    • Mentor project teams to drive adoption of new process and tools
    • Assist in planning and strategy of the Center of Capability
    • Help in creation of mentoring program vision and plans
    • Assist in process customization/ tailoring and tool configuration
    • Help identify projects and internal mentors for mentoring
    • Create "Mentoring Action Plan" and conduct regular mentoring sessions
    • Document and promote results
    Staffing
    Skills

    Technical skills:

    • Mentor is a L4/L5 practitioner in one or more tools

    Non-technical skills (People skill/soft skills):

    • Ability to mentor and coach
    • Leadership
    • Communication and collaboration
    Key Considerations
    Without leadership and mentoring skills, one cannot be effective in this role. Only a strong leader is effective in "leading change."
