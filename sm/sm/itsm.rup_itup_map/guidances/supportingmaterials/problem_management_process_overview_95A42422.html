ITUP Problem Management Process Overview
The Problem Management process is intended to minimize the adverse impacts and problems on the business that are caused by errors within the IT infrastructure, and to prevent recurrence of incidents related to these errors.

Main Description

This process is primarily concerned with establishing the root cause of an Incident and its subsequent resolution and prevention both reactively and proactively. Simply stated problem management is all about finding trends in incidents, grouping these incidents into problems, identifying the root causes of problems, and initiating one or more Request for Change (RFC) against those problems.

Goals

  • To minimize the number and adverse impact of incidents and problems
  • To prevent recurrence of incidents
  • To prevent potential incidents
  • To contributing to the effectiveness and efficiency of managing incidents
  • To improve the productivity of support staff.

Scope

The process is primarily concerned with establishing the underlying causes of an incident and their subsequent resolution and prevention. The reactive function is to solve problems relating to one or more incidents. The proactive function is to identify and solve problems before Incidents occur.

Includes

  • Root cause analysis and identification
  • Submission of Request for Change (RFC)
  • Appropriate prioritization of resources required for resolution based on business need
  • Contribution to the collective knowledge base

Excludes

  • Creation and resolution of incidents
  • Actual resolution of problems. Project Management initiates their resolution using Change Management and ensures that they are carried out.
  • Knowledge Management methodology. 

Key Performance Indicators

  • Number of known problems eliminated
  • Status of RFCs created to eliminate known problems
  • Historical number of incidents eliminated through problem elimination
  • Number of known errors (with workarounds) added to the known error database
  • Trend in number of incidents opened and closed
  • Percent of incidents related to known problems
  • Percent of incidents that are the result of known errors
  • The elapsed time and direct costs
    • In this process domain.
    • In each process step and between steps
