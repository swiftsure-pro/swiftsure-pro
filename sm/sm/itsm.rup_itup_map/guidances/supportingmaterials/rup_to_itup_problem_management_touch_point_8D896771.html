RUP to ITUP Problem Management Touch Point
This page provides an inside look at unifying the RUP Project Management discipline with the ITUP Problem Management process offering a heightened awareness of how software development processes affect and are affected by IT operations.
Main Description
The goal of this touch point is to expand RUP's coverage of the process of problem management in order to minimize the adverse impact of Incidents and Problems on the business that are caused by errors within the IT Infrastructure and to prevent the recurrence of incidents related to these errors.

Some facets of problem management are covered in the RUP  Project Management discipline, such as:

  •  Develop Problem Resolution Plan
  •  Handle Exceptions and Problems

However the content in RUP around problem management needs to be expanded to ensure more in-depth coverage so that the software developer:

  • understands the Problem Management process used in their environment
  • assists and facilitates root cause analysis
  • practices proactive problem management by redirecting their efforts from reacting to incidents to preventing incidents
  • identifies and resolves problems and Known Errors before incidents occur

The Capability Pattern: Problem Management, as depicted in Figure 1, with its set of activities and related tasks, work products and roles provides the software developer with expanded RUP coverage of the problem management process by augmenting the RUP tasks  Develop Problem Resolution Plan and  Handle Exceptions and Problems.

 

 Figure 1 - The Processes of Problem Management



For instance, let us consider the RUP task  Develop Problem Resolution Plan and its step Define Problem Resolution Procedure(s). This step states "The first step in developing your Problem Resolution Plan is to define the procedure to be followed for handling each category of problem". When you augment this task and this step with the ITUP  Task: Determine Process Procedures you have effectively expanded the coverage of  problem management in RUP.

Lets also take a look at the RUP task  Handle Exceptions and Problems. As part of the step Evaluate Exceptions and Problems we want to be proactive and avoid problems before they occur or at least to know about potential problems beforehand so that counter measures can be undertaken and service improvement recommendations forwarded. The ITUP activity Manage Problems Proactively offers process guidance in this area such as:

  • ensuring that trend analysis is performed on problem data and major incidents to proactively identify and resolve problems before incidents occur
  • technical documentation updates from vendor suppliers are periodically reviewed to determine if potential problems could occur with the existing infrastructure

As a result expanding the coverage of  problem management in RUP will effectively minimize the adverse impact of incidents and problems on the business that are caused by errors within the IT Infrastructure and prevent recurrence of incidents related to these errors. 

As a summary, here are the relationships between RUP and ITUP elements at touch point:

RUP Elements ITUP Elements Relationship

Discipline:  Project Management

  • Task:  Develop Problem Resolution Plan
  • Task:  Handle Exceptions and Problems

ITUP Problem Management Process Overview

  • Incidents
  • Problems
  • Known Error
  • Problem Management
    • Task:  Determine Process Procedures
    • Activity: Manage Problems Proactively
