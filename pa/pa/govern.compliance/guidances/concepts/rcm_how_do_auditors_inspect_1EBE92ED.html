This concept introduces audit and inspection and examines the process through which they are conducted.

Main Description
ยงThe term auditor is generally used to describe someone who is an authority in the applicable domain and has been hired by the business to conduct the business operations inspection.

The term inspector usually describes an authority from a government agency, typically from an enforcement unit that comes to perform the business operations inspection.

Auditors and inspectors typically seek the same types of information:

  • Documented processes for creation of all business artifacts
  • Adherence to the defined process by artifact creators
  • Integration between process support tools implementing tool-directed behavior (TDB)
  • Process automation supported by tool mentors
  • Artifact traceability to points of accountability in the delivery chain
  • Transparency of reporting
  • Accountability in the chain of delivery
  • Non-repudiation of any artifact throughout the system

Auditing and inspection is generally carried out by exception. A problem is identified, and investigation commences. When an exception occurs, auditors or inspectors begin to work backward from the exception to determine its root cause. Typically, these inspectors seek to determine if the exception is the result of:

  1. System design flaw
  2. Human error
  3. Malicious behavior

Documentation that auditors look for includes:

  • Evidence of a documented process
  • Evidence of process maturity and stability
  • Evidence of process compliance
  • Linkages between processes and artifacts
  • Documented linkage of test results to requirements
  • Documented and formalized handoffs and sign-offs
