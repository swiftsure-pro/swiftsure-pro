Checklist: Process Validation Plan
This checklist helps make sure that a Process Validation Plan is complete.
Relationships
Related Elements
  • Process Validation Plan
Main Description
Check Items
The process validation plan clearly identifies the scope of the effort

It must state the following:

  • In addition to the compliance requirements addressed by the project, what overvall properties of the process will be tested?
  • The specific aspects of tool-directed behavior addressed
  • Any assumptions, risks, or contingencies that may affect or impact the effort
The validation plan clearly identifies the artifacts (and versions) used to generate the contents of the plan
Each process compliance requirement has at least one associated requirement for validation, or a statement justifying why it is not needed
A clear and concise strategy is documented for each type of validation to be implemented and executed
For each validation strategy, the plan has the following information has been clearly stated:
  • The name of the test and its objective
  • A description of how the test will be implemented and executed
  • A description of the metrics, measurement methods, and criteria to be used to evaluate the quality of the target-of-test and completion of the test
All of the resources needed to successfully implement and execute testing have been identified, including hardware, software, and personnel
The plan contains a schedule or list of milestones identifying the major project- and test-related tasks (start and end dates, and effort)
The validation plan identifies the artifacts created by the validation tasks, when the artifacts are made available, how they will be distributed, their content, and how they should be used
