Introduction

This roadmap supplements the main software delivery roadmap in situations where a release includes Application Controls (requirements specific to compliance management). The additional activities involve creating the compliance-related work products, and subsequently verifying that the compliance requirements were met in a controlled fashion. To get the most out of this roadmap, it is strongly recommended as a pre-requisite that you read and understand the main software delivery roadmap.

Process enhancements

Compliance Management work products

The major addition covered by this roadmap results from the addition of a new controlled work product called Compliance Requirements. These are Application Controls whose purpose is to enhance The ability of IT organizations to achieve compliance. Compliance Requirements constitute a subset of all project artifacts, and it is added to the responsibilities of the requirements specifier. This artifact creates the need to introduce the following additional work products:

Corporate Compliance Policy -- This work product provides the organization's interpretation of the standards and regulations to which the organization is subject. It is necessary because regulations are often non-directive: While they tell you what you need to achieve, they don't tell you how to do it. It is developed by the Policy Analyst. Developing this artifact, however, is outside the scope of this plug-in. It is only used here as an input.

Compliance Enhancement Vision Addendum is a sub-artifact of the vision document that focuses on the aspects of the project intended to improve the control and auditability of the software development process. This artifact is a high-level outline of the compliance requirements, techniques, metrics (and so on) that are considered in the project, and it summarizes the approach for validating that the process meets these control objectives.

Compliance Test Plan is a sub-artifact of the Test Plan that focuses on verifying that compliance requirements have been met.

Work product control

The following additional approvals are now required, thereby making the associated artifacts controlled work products.

  • Compliance Vision Addendum Approval formalizes the validity of the approach being taken to manage compliance for the application or software product under development. It is the responsibility of the Compliance Analyst.
  • Compliance Requirements Approval formalizes agreement regarding the compliance requirements that will be incorporated into the application or software product. It is the responsibility of the Policy Analyst.
  • Test Plan Approval for Compliance formalizes that the project has appropriate test plans in place to verify the compliance requirements.

Key roles

Compliance Management introduces new roles, in part because of the new work products introduced, but also to address the separation of duties.

Compliance Management roles

Role: Compliance Analyst — Defines corporate Compliance Policy and captures compliance requirements (after cataloging and interpreting regulations, standards, and policies to identify internal policies that allow the business to be compliant). Creates traceability between the compliance requirements, the internal policies, and the appropriate regulations, standards, and policies.

Compliance related Roles

Requirements Specifier — existing RUP role who details the compliance requirements, establishes priorities, and defines workflow around the "in scope" requirements.

Software Development process

This process is an extension of the base process wherein additional activities take place to address the development of compliance software requirements (a.k.a. Term Definition: Application Control)

Inception Phase tasks

The two new tasks in the Inception Phase are those needed to develop compliance requirements:

  • Formulate Compliance Vision
    The Compliance Analyst produces a detailed outline of the compliance management requirements that will be incorporated during the project, and highlights the compliance-related goals within the project.
  •  Task: Detail the Software Requirements -- This task is a contribution to the RUP detail requirements task, with a focus on defining compliance requirements with the participation of the Compliance Analyst

There are a number of approvals introduced in the Inception Phase designed to ensure that the compliance requirements will be met. These include:

  • Approve Compliance Approach
    This task is closely coupled with the Formulate Compliance Vision. The purpose of this approval is to verify that the core compliance stakeholders accept the approach being used to incorporate compliance enhancements into the application. This will include acceptance of the compliance requirements in scope for the project, and their time line for delivery. The Compliance Analyst has authority for this approval.
  • Approve Test Approach
    This task is closely coupled with  Agree on the Mission. It provides a formal approval of the test plan designed to validate that the compliance management requirements have been included and satisfied. This approval is less formal, and is often approved internally to the team by the Test Manager.

Elaboration Phase Tasks

During the Elaboration Phase, the Architect begins to work on the critical scenarios that will have an impact on the architecture. The Implementer translates the design specification into the software product or application to automate IT Process Controls required for compliance. The Tester will define tests to validate that the IT Process Controls introduced in this phase are working successfully.

This phase is virtually identical to that of the main roadmap except that the compliance requirements will potentially be revised and hence re-approved in each iteration.

At the end of this phase, the compliance requirements should be stable, any architectural concerns should be resolved, and the chosen approach to introducing IT Process Controls should be proven in both the application and within the process.

Construction Phase Tasks

This phase is conducted according to the main software delivery roadmap without changes.

Transition Phase Tasks

This phase is conducted according to the main software delivery roadmap with added focus on final testing and deployment of the Application Control into the production environment.
