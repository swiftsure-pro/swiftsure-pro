Term Definition: Application Control
Main Description

This term denotes Business Controls implemented through application automation. These business controls are equivalent to software requirements that have regulatory or standards-based significance.

For example, a common business control that reduces risk to the Human Resources (HR) organization is to “Limit access to classified employee data.” This business control is automated by making it a common software requirement that applies to all HR systems. In this way, the organization can more consistently track and manage this control across its enterprise architecture.

Because application controls are of interest to auditors and other compliance stakeholders, these software requirements are separated out for unique reporting and traceability processes. Projects incorporating additional controls into existing processes and applications are referred to as Compliance Enhancement Projects
