Term Definition: Traceability
Main Description

This term denotes a property of a process whereby interdependent work products are explicitly related, and evidence of their consistency across the process can easily be produced. As complete traceability across all artifacts is not always required, different kinds of traceability are identified to focus on different aspects of system consistency. Following are examples of common types of traceability:

  • Procedural traceability: All required artifacts are produced in the right order (that is, all approvals exist, and approvals cannot predate the completion date for the approved work product)
  • Requirements Traceability: Features of the product are established through tests, and tests are linked to the requirements on which the system is based
  • Risk Traceability is a variant of the above, and establishes a link between risks and any aspect of the product designed to mitigate them
  • Production Code Traceability: All code elements can be traced to a change request or bug report
