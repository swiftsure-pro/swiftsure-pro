Term Definition: Compliant
Main Description

Being compliant is short for "being in compliance with a policy, regulation, or standard". In general, this implies the ability to substantiate meeting both performance and procedural requirements.

· Performance requirements demonstrate the ability to deliver functionality or perform tasks that support a specific policy. Examples of performance requirements might include the specific audit log data, or financial report content and format mandated by that policy.

· Procedural requirements demonstrate adherence to documented operational process (for example, “employers will be responsible for appropriately validating the identity of employees”).

Companies must rely on their own legal staffs and risk officers to interpret regulations and codify their interpretation into company policy.
