Task: Formulate Compliance Vision
This task produces an extension to the vision artifact, focusing on compliance management activities within the project. It also includes a description of the problem to be solved, the key stakeholders, the scope and boundary of the system, the system's key features, and any constraints.
Disciplines: rup_requirements_discipline
Purpose
  • To gain agreement on what problems need to be solved, based on compliance requirements
  • To identify stakeholders
  • To define compliance goals and strategy
  • To describe key features.

        Steps
        Review the corporate Compliance Policy and refine compliance requirements
        Compliance requirements have been outlined based on the corporate Compliance Policy and the compliance goals of the project. At this point, these requirements must be reviewed and refined to serve as a basis for the development of the compliance requirements.

        Sometimes, the group can be so focused on an envisioned solution that it is hard to get them to formulate what the underlying problem actually is. In such cases, it can be beneficial to explore the benefits of the solution, and then try to find the problems that those benefits will solve. You can then explore whether or not those problems are truly problems within the organization. Techniques commonly used to find the problem behind the problem are  brainstorming,  Fishbone Diagrams, and  Pareto Diagrams.

        Identify Stakeholders

        Depending on the domain expertise of the development team, identifying the stakeholders may be a trivial or a nontrivial step. Often, this simply involves interviewing decision-makers, potential users, and other interested parties. The following questions are helpful:

        • Who are the users of the system or process that we are enhancing?
        • Who will be affected by the outputs that the system or process produces?
        • Who will evaluate and approve the system or process when it is delivered and deployed?
        • Are there any other internal or external users of the system or process whose needs must be addressed?
        • Who maintains the system or process?
        • Is there anyone else that we should consider?

        Start to develop profiles of potential (or actual) users. Initial information about key users and their environments should be documented in Artifact: Compliance Vision Addendum.

        Define boundaries

        The system or process boundary defines the border between the solution and the real world that surrounds the solution. In other words, the system boundary describes an envelope in which the solution system is contained. Information, in the form of inputs and outputs, is passed back and forth from the system to the users outside of the system. All interactions with the system occur through interfaces between the system and the external world.

        • In the event that the compliance enhancement project focuses on enhancing IT systems by implementing Term Definition: Application Control the requirements are usually a subset of all systems.
        • The same observation is applicable to corporate processes, because individual projects will target only a subset of these processes.
        Identify constraints to be imposed on the system

        There are a variety of sources of constraints to consider.Here is a list of potential sources and questions to ask:

        • Political: Are there internal or external political issues that affect potential solutions? Interdepartmental issues?
        • Economic: Which financial or budgetary constraints are applicable? Are there costs of goods sold or product pricing considerations? Are there any licensing issues?
        • Environmental: Are there environmental or regulatory constraints? Legal? Other standards that create constraints?
        • Technical: Are we restricted in our choice of technologies? Are we constrained to work within existing platforms or technologies? Are we prohibited from using any new technologies?
        • Feasibility: Is the schedule defined? Are we restricted to existing resources? Can we use outside labor? Can we expand resources? If so, must they be temporary or can they be permanent?
        • System: Is the solution to be built on our existing systems? Must we maintain compatibility with existing solutions? Which operating systems and environments must be supported?
        • Process: How much of existing processes must be maintained and enhanced as opposed to, potentially, fully re-engineered? What tools will be available for purchase or use to automate the process effectively?
        Formulate compliance goals

        With the whole group, work on easel charts or whiteboards and fill in the following template for each standard, regulation, or policy that you have targeted for compliance:

        The decision to comply with <standard, regulation, policy>
        is necessitated by <business or regulatory reasons>.
        A successful solution will strive to <list key goals of a successful solution>
        And would result in the following <list benefits and any impact on the organization>

        The purpose of this template is to help you distinguish rules from goals that the organization has set to comply with the rules and from the changes that will support the goals.

        Define features of the system or strategies identified for process enhancement

        Based on the benefits listed in your problem statements, develop a list of features that you want in the system or process. Describe them briefly, and give them attributes to help define their general states and priorities in the project.

        Evaluate your results

        Check the Compliance Vision Addendum at this stage to verify that your work is on track, but you do not need to review it in detail. Instead, consider using the checklist for that purpose.
