Main Description

Electronic signatures can support key control points in the development process. An electronic signature can serve as the equivalent of a written signature in an official capacity.

Before affixing a signature to a control point (such as a test result, an approval, or a decision), the signer's identity is authenticated. Only after the signer has passed an authentication test can the signer affix his or her signature to the electronic form. When the form is signed, its contents are either secured -- and users are prohibited from further edits-- or further edits are recorded in an audit log for auditor inspection. Electronic signatures serve as the legal equivalent of a written signature on a physical document.
