Concept: External Business Factors Impacting Compliance Initiatives
This concept presents key examples of the external business factors that impact the ability of an organization to respond to a new requirement.

Main Description

The business environment has a direct impact on a company’s ability to invest in compliance initiatives. Although it is not exhaustive, the list that follows provides a meaningful sample of external conditions and their potential impact on compliance initiatives:

Competitive landscape: Business conditions affect the budget. The budget, in turn, affects a company’s material ability to implement changes to business processes and systems. If the profit margin is small, containment of cost is imperative.

Mergers and acquisitions: Integrating companies from mergers and acquisitions (M&A) compounds the complexities of a compliance management program. Companies involved in M&A should establish standard operating procedures to improve compliance management.

Outsourcing of development: In many cases, the company producing the product or service remains accountable for the product's compliance with regulations or standards. This holds even if some or all of its process has been outsourced. A practical solution to this problem is to require the use of standard tooling during the RFP process for outsourcing contracts. Requiring contractors to use the contracting company’s standardized tooling helps ensure that the completed application designs and artifact traceability will be usable as evidence when the final application is delivered. 

Maintenance of COT customizations: Management of COTS (Commercial Off-the-Shelf) applications is just as important from a compliance standpoint as green field systems development. In many cases, the applications created today are the result of aggregation efforts across multiple COTS applications. Significant work must often be performed to ensure that you:

  • Verify that the COT applications function appropriately
  •  Understand the gaps between the needs of the business and its policies and to understand the capabilities of the COT applications under consideration.
