Concept: Audit Trail
An audit trail provides a record of the changes made to a work product.

Main Description

An audit trail provides a record of the changes made to a work product that are subject to audits (also known as controlled work products). For example, all change requests within a project should have an audit trail for compliance purposes.

Typically, the audit trail captures a record of the change and the specifies:

  • Who changed it
  • When it was changed: Capturing the date, time, and time zone of the change.
  • The action and state: The action that changed the work product.
  • Deleted work products: Information is never deleted from the audit trail. If a work product subject to audits is deleted, an entry is made in the audit trail showing the deleted work product.
  • What fields were changed: For each data field in the work product that was changed with this event, the audit trail will record the name of the field, its old value, and its new value.

The Change Manager specifies what work products should automatically have an audit trail.
