Concept: Process Control for Compliance-Minded Change Management
This concept reviews the key elements that need to be considered for change management compliance.

Main Description

Here are a number of process control practices to consider for compliance-minded change management:

  • All changes to Controlled Work Products must be documented and, ultimately, approved by management.
  • Traceability across controlled work products must be established and maintained. 
  • Sufficient metadata must be collected to assist in conducting an impact analysis that will be captured within a change request.
  • Changes will be managed from inception to transition.
  • The process will be designed to support Separation of Duties.
  • Audit Trails will be used for compliance documentation. They include information such as who changed what, where, and when and can be easily captured through automated tooling.
  • Electronic Signatures will be used to allow the enforcement of controls through electronic sign-off.
