Guideline: Controlling Records
This guidelines describes what must be done for identifying and applying controls to records.

Main Description

Records are soft- or hard-copy documents stating results achieved or providing evidence of activities performed. Whereas controlled documents can evolve over time, records are static in the sense that they can never be modified once they are created. Records need to be identified and controlled in order to provide evidence that demonstrates the effective, ongoing unfolding of compliant software projects. Records must be identified (listed) in the Record Control Specification .

To address the eventuality that the role responsible for a record is unavailable, or that the authorized individual causes a conflict with Separation of Duties, delegation must be added to the process. This can be achieved by either one of the following:

  • Expanding the Record Control Specification to include a delegation scheme based on the automatic selection of alternate roles
  • Creating a delegation process in which all roles with record ownership must participate, and where they name one or more alternates

Requirements:

The following list identifies the basic, minimum requirements that must be applied for controlling records:

  1. Records that provide evidence of conformity to compliance requirements and effective project operation must be identified.
  2. Records must be stored under controlled conditions to safeguard them from intentional or accidental loss or changes.
  3. Records must be easily accessible to all who need them.
  4. Records must be retained for a minimum, specified length of time, as exemplified in the "Record Retention and Disposition" table specified in the record control specification. Guidelines on the contents of that table are provided in the table below.
  5. At the end of the retention period, the record may be disposed of or deleted.

Which Records to Control:

All records necessary to the effective management of the project, or that are mandated by compliance requirements, must be controlled. They include, for example, approvals and any review of all controlled work products. Additional records may be identified based on specific project, business, or contractual needs and customer requirements.
