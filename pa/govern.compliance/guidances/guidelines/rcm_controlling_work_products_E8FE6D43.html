Guideline: Controlling Work Products
This guideline describes what must be done to apply controls to work products..
Main Description

The work products to be controlled are those that have an impact on the effectiveness of the aspects of the process subject to compliance management. They must have controls applied to them in order to ensure and demonstrate that all affected parties can work from the same official (approved) and current versions of information, and that processes are used in a consistent and repeatable fashion.

Controlled work product properties

The following list identifies the basic, minimum requirements that controlled work products must have:

  • Prior to issue, the work product must be approved by the designated approver in order to confirm its adequacy for use (the approval often automatically results in the issue). The designated work product approver(s) cannot be the work product author(s).
  • Work product status (such as draft, current, obsolete, or archive) must be shown.
  • Work product must specify the name of the designated owner and author.
  • The history of significant work product changes must be summarized and identified.
  • The current revision status must be shown by including a unique version label, incremental version number, or the date issued.
  • Suitable identification must be applied to obsolete work product if it is retained for any purpose.
    For example: "the work product is archived for customer support purposes"
  • Work product, in its approved form, cannot contain strikeouts, review comments, or unresolved issues (resolution can be to not consider issue until a later stage in the project).
  • Work product must be legible, readily identifiable, complete, and easily retrievable.

Controlled work product management guidelines

General guidelines
  • For work product review and approval records, electronic signatures are the equivalent of handwritten signatures.
  • Changes that are cosmetic in nature (such as correcting grammar, formatting, spelling errors, or work product links or URLs) do not require reapproval. Minor changes that do not change the work product meaning or content require no reapproval as well.
  • The current, approved versions of work products should be available for use wherever needed.
  • All controlled work products must be catalogued through an official list (for example, the list of work product approval records defined in the Record Control Specification).
  • Any work products in use having an external (non-project) origin must be identified to ensure that current versions are used and, for copyrighted work products, their distribution controlled.
  • The use of any obsolete work products must be prevented, unless intended.
  • For forms, templates, checklists, and schedules, the control required is only a unique version label, version number, or revision date.
Which work products to control:

The general guideline is to strongly consider control a work product if it:

  • Is required to produce the offering
  • Is used by multiple people who all need to work from the same level of information or procedure
  • Contains key or vital business, offering, or customer information
  • Significantly influences the quality of the offering

The following list identifies the types of work products that must be strongly considered for control:

  • Method and process elements
  • Guidance
  • Plans and schedules
  • Designs, specifications, and requirements
  • Operations manuals
  • Internal standards
  • External documents (such as standards, copyrighted or legal documents, or customer-supplied documents)
  • Contracts or other formal agreements
Work product review and approval

The control of work product should be based on the workflow defined in Capability Pattern: Develop a Controlled Work Product and analyzed in Mapping the RASCI Model to UMA for Work Product Control. In addition to identifying the key Approving role, this workflow describes how other roles support the approval process in a way consistent with separation of concerns. In addition, the approval process is subject to the following guidelines.

  1. Work product approval denotes that the designated approver(s) has read, understands and agrees with the content of the controlled work product or has taken appropriate action (e.g. mandate reviews) to ascertain its quality. Controlled work products can be approved with or without comments.
  2. Requests for work product reviews and/or approvals must clearly identify the work product to be reviewed and/or approved.
  3. For electronic reviews and approvals of controlled work products, the records of work product review and/or approval must contain the complete user id of the required reviewer(s) and/or designated approver(s), and the date and time stamp of their review and/or approval.
  4. For hardcopy reviews and approvals of controlled work products, the records of work product review and/or approval must contains the reviewer(s) and/or designated approver(s) wet-ink signature and the date of their signature.
  5. In a case of work product approval or review without comments and/concerns, the approver's or reviewer's response is usually deemed sufficient. While helpful, other supporting information (such as the basis for the approval) is usually not required. No form of response is required from optional reviewers.
